## Prompt

/sp.constitution Hackathon I: Create a Textbook for Teaching Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics

Preamble
This constitution establishes the guiding principles, academic mandate, and structural foundations for the textbook developed for the course in Physical AI and Humanoid Robotics. As global workforces transition toward hybrid collaboration between humans, intelligent agents, and robotic systems, the purpose of this textbook is to provide a rigorous, future-ready pedagogical framework that empowers learners to acquire the competencies required in emerging fields of embodied AI.

Article I. Purpose

1. To define a comprehensive, interdisciplinary curriculum that introduces students to the principles, methods, and applications of Physical AI and Humanoid Robotics.
2. To integrate theoretical foundations with hands-on, project-driven learning that equips students to design, build, and deploy intelligent physical systems.
3. To cultivate systems thinking, engineering literacy, and ethical awareness in the development of advanced robotic platforms and autonomous agents.
4. To contribute to the global discourse on next-generation skills required in a world where human intelligence is augmented by artificial and robotic intelligence.

Article II. Scope

1. The textbook covers the conceptual, mathematical, computational, and mechanical fundamentals of Physical AI.
2. Core domains include sensing, actuation, control systems, cognition, human-robot interaction, humanoid robotics, safety, alignment, and responsible innovation.
3. Supplementary domains include applied robotics for industry, research methodologies, simulation workflows, and cross-disciplinary integrations with design, biology, and cognitive science.
4. All content is designed to serve undergraduate, postgraduate, and professional learners seeking competency in robotics and embodied intelligence.

Article III. Learning Framework

1. Curriculum chapters shall be organized to progress from foundational concepts to advanced implementations.
